About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Physical Chemistry at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in the Landes Research Group, working on 6D single-molecule orientation and localization microscopy (6D-SMOLM). My research focuses on developing advanced optical microscopy techniques that simultaneously determine both 3D spatial positions and 3D molecular orientations of fluorescent molecules.

I develop custom microscopy systems using phase engineering with liquid crystal spatial light modulators (SLMs) and polarization-resolved detection. My work combines vectorial diffraction theory simulations with deep learning frameworks to extract molecular dynamics information in low signal-to-noise conditions, with applications to battery technology, ion transport in polymer electrolytes, and pH-responsive polymer brushes.

Current Projects

6D Optical Microscopy for Single-Molecule Tracking

Developed a custom 6D optical microscope using phase engineering and a liquid crystal spatial light modulator (SLM) to extract 3D positions and 3D orientations of single molecules. Built full instrument workflows from hardware alignment to vectorial diffraction theory simulations in MATLAB and trained deep learning models on simulated microscopy data to estimate molecular orientations in low SNR conditions.

D-blur: Deep Learning for Motion-Blurred PSFs

Co-developed D-blur, a deep learning method to analyze motion-blurred point spread functions, enabling accurate tracking of fast diffusion dynamics in single-molecule microscopy. Created a machine learning algorithm to classify single-molecule trajectories (Brownian, directed, anomalous, confined transport) with 80% accuracy.

Ion Transport in Polymer Electrolytes

Applied fluorescence microscopy to study ion transport mechanisms in polymer electrolytes and pH-responsive polymer brushes to advance battery technology. Aligned optical microscopes for simultaneous tracking of orientation of anionic dyes in polyethylene oxide films. Contributed to DOE proposal development for investigating fouling mechanisms in ion-exchange membranes.

Antibody Dynamics in 3D

Investigating spatial and orientational antibody dynamics in three dimensions using advanced single-molecule tracking techniques, combining position and orientation information to understand molecular behavior at interfaces.
